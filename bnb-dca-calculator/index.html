Why DCA into BNB?

BNB (Binance Coin) is unique among major cryptocurrencies — it has real utility built into one of the world's largest crypto ecosystems. Holding BNB gives you discounts on Binance trading fees, powers transactions on BNB Chain (formerly BSC), and is used in Binance's various products and launches.

Beyond utility, Binance conducts quarterly token burns — using a portion of its profits to permanently destroy BNB tokens. This deflationary mechanism has supported BNB's price over the long term. DCA investors in BNB benefit from both price appreciation and reduced supply over time.

BNB price history highlights

  • 2019: BNB rose from ~$5 to $40 as Binance expanded globally
  • 2020: DeFi summer drove BNB to $30 range
  • 2021 peak: BNB hit $690 as Binance Smart Chain attracted massive DeFi activity
  • 2022 correction: BNB dropped to ~$200 during the crypto bear market
  • 2024 recovery: BNB recovered strongly, driven by continued Binance ecosystem growth

BNB DCA vs lump sum

Like all crypto assets, BNB's volatility makes timing the market difficult. Buying the 2021 top with a lump sum would have meant a painful waiting period. DCA investors who kept buying through 2022 accumulated BNB at prices averaging well below the peak — and benefited from the recovery.

Frequently asked questions

Is BNB a good coin to DCA?

BNB has utility backing from the Binance ecosystem and a deflationary token burn schedule. These fundamentals make it a popular DCA choice among Binance users.

What is the BNB quarterly burn?

Every quarter, Binance uses 20% of its profits to buy and permanently destroy BNB tokens. This reduces the total supply over time, creating deflationary pressure on price.
