Solana's dramatic price history

Solana emerged as one of the most exciting blockchains of the 2021 bull run — fast, cheap, and technically impressive. SOL rose from under $2 in early 2020 to an all-time high of $260 in November 2021. Then came one of crypto's hardest lessons.

The FTX collapse in November 2022 devastated Solana — FTX's founder Sam Bankman-Fried was one of SOL's biggest backers. The price crashed below $10. Many investors panic-sold. Those who kept DCA'ing through the crash bought large amounts of SOL at single-digit prices, and were rewarded when SOL recovered above $200 in 2024.

Why DCA works especially well for Solana

SOL is one of the most volatile major cryptocurrencies. That volatility cuts both ways — massive crashes and massive recoveries. A DCA strategy:

  • Prevents putting all your money in at the November 2021 top
  • Automatically accumulates more SOL during the 2022 crash at $10–$20
  • Turns bear market fear into opportunity
  • Removes the emotional decision-making that causes most investors to buy high and sell low
